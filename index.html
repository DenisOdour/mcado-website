About MCADO

Mathare Community Anti-Drugs Organization (MCADO)is a grassroots-based, non-profit, and non-political community-based organization established in 2018. It was founded by concerned community leaders from Mathare who were trained at the Kenya Government School through a partnership with CADCA (Community Anti-Dreg Coalitions of America), an American organization specializing in community-based strategies for preventing alcohol, drug, and substance misuse.

Mission

To mitigate alcohol and drug abuse through prevention, education, and community sensitization by uniting stakeholders in the fight against substance misuse.

Vision

A healthy and drug-free community where individuals, especially youth, can thrive in safe and supportive environments.

Our Approach

MCADO employs a holistic, community-driven approach to prevent and reduce drug and substance abuse, focusing on rehabilitation, empowerment, education, and sustained recovery. This approach is implemented through our two core thematic areas: Community Dialogue & Prevention, and Community Empowerment & Recovery Support.

1. Community Dialogue & Prevention

This pillar focuses on proactive, inclusive engagement to raise awareness, prevent early initiation, and build community resilience.

School-Based Prevention

We implement school-based interventions through structured clubs that promote drug awareness, life skills, and leadership among students. The program encourages open discussions, mentorship, and creative expression to prevent youth from engaging in drugs and risky behaviors.

Community Outreach and Dialogue Forums

Through community dialogues, inter-generational forums, and stakeholder engagement, we bring together youth, elders, religious leaders, boda boda operators, teachers, and local administration to address social issues such as drug abuse, unemployment, and insecurity. These forums build solidarity and lead to the formation of peer-support structures that reinforce positive behavior.

2. Community Empowerment & Recovery Support

This thematic area provides targeted support to individuals in recovery and vulnerable groups, emphasizing rehabilitation, economic empowerment, and social reintegration.

Community-Based Rehabilitation and Aftercare

Our rehabilitation model is grounded in social and human rights principles, focusing on dignity, inclusion, and community participation. Aftercare services provide ongoing support to help individuals maintain sobriety, strengthen personal growth, and reconnect with their families and communities. Peer mentoring and support groups play a vital role in sustaining recovery.

Socio-Economic Empowerment Programs

To reduce relapse and promote independence, MCADO offers skill-building programs tailored to different vulnerable groups:

  • Youth Empowerment through Urban Farming:Engaging young men in smart agriculture and environmental restoration for food sustainability and income generation.
  • Entrepreneurship for Young Mothers: Providing training in baking, T-shirt printing, leatherwork, and eco-friendly crafts, paired with mentorship and financial literacy.
  • Climate Resilience for Older Adults:Empowering parents and grandparents through psychosocial support, urban gardening, and climate education.

Our Major Pillars

1. Prevention and Education

This pillar focuses on reducing drug and substance abuse before it starts through awareness and behavior change initiatives.
Key Activities:

(a) Community outreach and sensitization
(b) School-based awareness sessions.
(c) Community dialogues with duty bearers
(d) Campaign on alcohol, drugs, HIV/AIDS, and crime prevention
(e) Training on lifeskills, decision-making, and resilience.

Impact:

(a)Increased awareness of risks associated with drug use.
(b) Positive behavior change among youth and families
(c) Strengthened community knowledge and responsibility

2. Livelihood Empowerment

This pillar addresses the root causes of drug abuse, such as poverty and unemployment by equiping beneficiaries with income-generating skills.

Key Activities:
(a)Digital literacy training(Computer skills, online safety)
(b)Vocational skills (e.g, tailoring, beadwork, printing)
(c)Entrepreneurship and small business training
(d)Mentorship and career guidance
(e)Support for youth and young mothers to start income-generating activities.

Impact:

(a)Increased economic independence
(b)Reduced vulnerability to drugs and crime
(c)Improved livelihoods for youth and young mothers

3. School Anti-Drugs Clubs

This pillar targets early prevention among school-going children by creating structured platforms for learning and peer support.

Key Activities:

(a) Formation of anti-drug clubs in schools
(b) Peer education and mentorship programs
(c) Debates, drama, and awareness campaigns
(d) Engagement with teachers and parents
(e) Life skills and leadership development

Impact:

(a) Early prevention of drug and substance abuse among school-going children.
(b) Development of peer role models.
(c) Improved school retention and discipline

4. Sports to Kick Out Drugs

This pillar uses sports as a tool for engagement, prevention, and talent development. this is always carried out during school holidays.

Key Activities:

(a)Football tournaments and leagues
(b)Sport-based mentorship and coaching
(c)Integration of awareness messages during sports events
(d) Talent identification and development
(e)Community engagement through sports events

Impact:

(a)Reduced idleness and exposure to risky behaviors
(b)Increased youth participation in positive activities
(c)platform for delivering drug prevention messages

Our Programs

Program 1: Community Dialogues for Drug Abuse Prevention and Social Empowerment

Target Group: Youth, Community elders, boda boda riders, teachers, and faith-based groups.

Goal: Strengthen communal bonds and address underlying social issues such as drug abuse

This program creates inclusive spaces for cross-generational dialogue on issues such as drug use, unemployment, violence, and moral erosion. It encourages open communication, breaking taboos and fostering trust aming stakeholders. These disscusion often lead to collective community action and formation of grassroots initiatives.

Activities Include:

  • Regular community forums led by trained facilitators and local leaders.
  • Inter-generational story telling and experience-sharing session.
  • Formation of localized peer support and recovery groups for youth and adults.
  • Door-to-door outreach and mobilization campaigns targeting high-risk areas
  • Involvement of religious and cultural institutions to foster shared values and moral guidance
  • Thematic dialogues focusing on drug abuse, unemployment, family dynamics, and civic responsibility.

Program2: School Clubs for Drug Awareness,Life Skills, and Positive Expression

Target Group:School-going children and adolescents

Goal:: Prevent early initiation into drug use and foster life skills, creativity, and leadership

MCADO works closely with schools to build student-run clubs that provide a safe and engaging environment to explore life challenges. The clubs encourage self-expression, peer learning, and leadership while delivering vital education on drug prevention and mental wellness.

Activities Include:

  • Interactive sessions on drug abuse, peer pressure, and mental health awareness
  • Leadership training and mentorship from MCADO role models and educators
  • Creative arts projects including poetry, skits, painting, and music as tools of expression
  • Conflict resolution, emotional intelligence, and decision-making workshops
  • School garden projects promoting environmental responsibility
  • Friendly inter-school debates, exhibitions, and talent showcases promoting positive competition

Program 1: Youth Empowerment through Urban Farming and Green Skills

Target Group: Young Men and Boys in Mathare and people with disability

Goal: Reduce drug dependency and unemployment by equipping youth with practical, eco-friendly livelihood skills.

This program transforms idle and polluted public spaces along the Mathare River into productive urban farms, offering young men sustainable alternatives to drugs and crime. Participants learn to grow food using innovative, space-efficient methods while receiving mentorship in leadership, teamwork, and personal development. By participating in peer-led support groups, many youth in recovery gain a sense of purpose and social belonging

Activities Include:

  • Establishment of micro-greenhouses and rooftop gardens
  • Income generation through selling vegetables, herbs, and seedlings to local markets
  • Peer mentorship and life coaching for at-risk youth and those recovering from substance abuse
  • Environmental stewardship and leadership workshops
  • Tree planting and environmental clean-up campaigns led by youth groups

Program 2: Economic Empowerment for Young Mothers

Target Group: Young mothers in informal settlements

Goal: Break cycles of poverty, dependency, and vulnerability through practical skills and green entrepreneurship.

Young mothers face unique socio-economic hardships, often compounded by limited education, abuse, and stigma. This program provides vocational training alongside emotional and financial empowerment. It builds participants' capacity to start small eco-friendly businesses while helping them recover from trauma and gain confidence as community contributors.

Activities Include:

  • Vocational skills training in baking, leatherwork, tailoring, and T-shirt printing
  • Entrepreneurship workshops using recyclable and eco-friendly materials
  • Financial literacy classes covering budgeting, savings, and digital payment systems
  • Access to micro-grants and cooperative saving schemes
  • Therapeutic support groups to address trauma, addiction, gender-based violence, and parenting stress
  • Participation in community fairs, exhibitions, and digital sales platforms to market products

Program 3: Mental Health and Climate Resilience for Older Adults

Target Group:Parents, grandparents, and older adults in Mathare

Goal: Promote mental well-being, reduce isolation and substance dependency, and empower older adults to lead in environmental action.

Older adults often feel left behind in urban informal settlements. This program restores their agency by providing mental health support, reconnecting them to community roles, and training them in climate resilience practices. Through this, they become not only beneficiaries but change agents, guiding youth and modeling sustainable living.

Activities Include:

  • Group counseling and community therapy sessions focused on grief, stress, and addiction
  • Workshops on mindfulness, self-care, and emotional intelligence
  • Training in home-based smart gardening (kitchen gardens, sack gardens)
  • Climate change awareness sessions tailored for the older generation
  • Mentorship roles for elders in youth farming and resilience projects
  • Story-sharing and cultural exchange forums between older and younger community members.

Guiding youth through life decisions and positive growth.
